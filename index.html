üìä Methodology & Tax Calculations

What is "Tax Burden"?

For this calculator, "tax burden" refers to the total amount of state-level taxes you would pay annually, including income tax, sales tax, and property tax. This gives you a comprehensive view of your total state tax liability.

Taxes Included in Our Comparison

Our calculator considers the three major state-level taxes that most individuals encounter:

üí∞ State Income Tax

Calculation: Annual Income √ó State Income Tax Rate

Data Source: State tax brackets and rates as of 2025

Assumption: We use the highest marginal rate for simplicity. Actual rates may vary based on income brackets and filing status.

What's NOT included: Federal income tax, local income taxes, or state-specific deductions and credits

üõí State Sales Tax

Calculation: (Annual Income √ó 30%) √ó State Sales Tax Rate

Assumption: 30% of annual income is spent on taxable goods and services

What's NOT included: Local sales tax variations, exempt items (groceries, prescription drugs), or online purchases

Note: Many states have varying local sales tax rates. We use state-level rates only.

üè† Property Tax

Calculation: (Annual Income √ó 25%) √ó State Property Tax Rate

Assumption: Property value is approximately 25% of annual income

What's NOT included: Local property tax variations, homestead exemptions, or special assessments

Note: Property tax rates vary by county and municipality. We use state averages.

Key Assumptions & Limitations

  • Filing Status: Assumes single filer with no dependents
  • Deductions: Uses standard deduction; no itemized deductions or credits
  • Income Distribution: Assumes income is evenly earned throughout the year
  • Simplified Rates: Uses flat rates rather than progressive tax brackets for income tax
  • Local Variations: Local taxes, fees, and special assessments are not included
  • Dynamic Rates: Tax rates change frequently and may differ from our data
  • Property Ownership: Assumes you own property; renters would have different tax implications

When to Use This Calculator

This tool is best used for:

  • General Planning: Getting a rough idea of tax differences between states
  • Comparison Shopping: Identifying states with generally higher or lower tax burdens
  • Initial Research: Starting point for more detailed tax planning

When NOT to Use This Calculator

This tool is NOT suitable for:

  • Precise Tax Planning: Final tax decisions or detailed financial planning
  • Complex Situations: Business owners, multiple income sources, or complex deductions
  • Legal Advice: Tax compliance or legal tax matters

Why These Assumptions?

We use these simplified assumptions to provide a quick, comparable estimate across states. While not perfect, this approach helps identify states with generally higher or lower tax burdens for planning purposes.
